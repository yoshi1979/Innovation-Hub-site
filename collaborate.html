Microsoft Product Groups

Co-Build & Integration Collaboration

Microsoft's Product Groups build the products that run the world's enterprises — Azure, Microsoft 365, Dynamics 365, Power Platform, GitHub, and more. The Hub's Co-Build Program creates a structured pathway for external companies to explore co-product collaboration with these teams — not just integration, but joint product development and co-marketing.

This is the most differentiated capability the Hub offers. No other startup program in the market provides a structured channel to explore joint product work with Microsoft's own engineering teams.

What collaboration is possible

  • Joint feature exploration with specific Microsoft PG teams (Azure, M365, Dynamics, GitHub Copilot)
  • Co-product integration that ships in both companies' roadmaps
  • Early access to private APIs and pre-GA platform features
  • Co-marketing & joint go-to-market for integrated solutions
  • Technical deep-dives with Microsoft's own architects and PMs

Microsoft CTO Group

Architecture Advisory & Executive Peer Access

The Microsoft CTO Group Israel is a small, senior team of technical leaders embedded within Microsoft whose mandate is to engage externally with the most technically ambitious companies in Israel's ecosystem. They sit at the intersection of Microsoft product strategy and Israeli tech innovation.

Hub companies access the CTO Group through two channels: the CTO Circle (peer executive sessions) and individual architecture advisory engagements coordinated by the Hub team.

What collaboration is possible

  • 1:1 architecture review sessions with a Microsoft CTO Group advisor (up to 4 per program cycle)
  • Access to Microsoft's internal technology radar and forward-looking platform direction
  • CTO Circle peer learning sessions: quarterly dinners + monthly virtual roundtables
  • Technical due diligence support ahead of major funding rounds
  • Introduction to Microsoft's global CTO network for enterprise deal support

Business Development

Strategic Partnerships & Commercial Deal Support

Microsoft's Business Development team in Israel operates at the intersection of strategic deal-making, commercial partnerships, and market expansion. For Hub companies at Series C/D and beyond, BD collaboration can be the difference between a regional deal and a global one.

BD collaboration is triggered when a Hub company has a strategic opportunity that benefits both parties — a large enterprise deal, a market expansion requiring Microsoft commercial backing, or an acquisition-relevant conversation.

What collaboration is possible

  • Strategic account introductions via Microsoft's enterprise BD relationships
  • Joint deal structuring for large enterprise contracts requiring Microsoft involvement
  • Market expansion support (EMEA, US) leveraging Microsoft's regional BD teams
  • M&A-adjacent conversations for companies on an acquisition path

Industry Solutions Engineering (ISE)

Embedded Engineering Collaboration

ISE is Microsoft's elite engineering collaboration team. Unlike a consultancy or an SA engagement, ISE engineers embed directly into Hub companies for sprint-based technical work — building alongside your team, not just advising it.

ISE engagements are time-boxed, outcome-focused, and delivered by senior engineers with deep Azure product expertise. They're responsible for some of the most technically transformative outcomes in Hub alumni history — including Lumina AI's enterprise-grade AI architecture rebuild.

What collaboration is possible

  • Sprint-based embedded engineering: 2–8 week focused build sprints
  • Architecture redesign for enterprise scalability, security, or compliance
  • Azure OpenAI / AI Studio integration — production-grade, not prototype
  • Multi-tenant SaaS refactoring for enterprise-grade isolation
  • Performance, observability, and reliability engineering
  • Azure Marketplace technical readiness preparation

GPS & Azure Marketplace

Co-Sell, GTM Engine & Marketplace Listing

GPS (Global Partner Solutions) is Microsoft's engine for commercial partner success. For Hub companies, a GPS co-sell relationship means your product appears in Microsoft's field sales motion — giving you access to Microsoft's 60,000-person global sales organization as a distribution channel.

Azure Marketplace is the commerce layer. A transactable Marketplace listing with co-sell designation is the single highest-leverage distribution decision most Hub companies will make.

What collaboration is possible

  • Co-sell ready designation: your offer becomes visible to Microsoft field teams
  • Azure IP Co-sell: unlocks incentives for Microsoft sellers who close deals with your product
  • Azure Marketplace listing: transactable offers, private plans, and MACC-eligible listings
  • Marketplace GTM Bootcamp: Hub-run, 1-day immersive preparation program
  • GPS partner manager assignment for Marketplace-active Hub companies

Solution Technical Unit (STU)

Pre-Sales Technical Validation & Deal Support

STU is Microsoft's deep technical pre-sales team — the people enterprise customers call when they need architecture-level answers before committing to a major deployment. For Hub companies, access to STU expertise means your product can be validated and championed inside large enterprise deals where Microsoft is already present.

STU engagement is particularly valuable when a Hub company is in a complex enterprise RFP or needs a trusted Microsoft technical voice to help close a skeptical CTO or CISO.

What collaboration is possible

  • Architecture validation sessions with Microsoft STU specialists (pre-sales support)
  • Enterprise deal support: STU joins as a technical co-presenter on your behalf
  • Security and compliance review (CISO-level conversations)
  • Reference architecture creation for joint customer proposals
  • Access to Azure Expert MSP and specialist community for delivery confidence

  • →A deal is stalled on a security or compliance objection you need Microsoft to address
  • →Your prospect needs a Microsoft-validated architecture before procurement approval
  • →You're building a strategic reference customer and need a credible co-deliverer

Customer Success Unit (CSU)

Post-Sale Technical Success & Adoption

CSU is Microsoft's post-sale technical success team. For Hub companies deploying joint solutions with Microsoft enterprise customers, CSU involvement ensures that the technical deployment succeeds — which means your mutual customer stays, expands, and advocates.

CSU collaboration also accelerates time-to-value for new Hub company deployments, reducing churn risk on large deals where implementation complexity is a factor.

What collaboration is possible

  • Joint onboarding plans for shared enterprise customers
  • CSU-backed deployment support for complex Azure + partner solution integrations
  • Health checks and adoption reviews at 30/90/180-day milestones
  • Technical escalation path via Microsoft's CSU management for critical deployments
  • NPS and renewal support for major joint accounts
