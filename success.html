CloudBridge: From AWS-Only to $12M Series C

$12MSeries C raised
23Enterprise accounts in 6 mo.
$2.8MAzure Marketplace ARR

CloudBridge came to the Hub as a Series A company with a world-class multi-cloud management product — and zero Microsoft footprint. Their entire GTM was AWS-native, their customer base were AWS-heavy enterprises, and their team had almost no Azure expertise.

Through the Scale360 program, CloudBridge was matched with a GPS co-sell manager (Dana Levi, Microsoft Israel GPS) who redesigned their sales motion to include Azure Marketplace as a primary channel. Simultaneously, their engineering team went through a 3-week ISV Excellence sprint where they added Azure-native support to their product.

Within six months of joining the Hub, CloudBridge had closed 23 enterprise accounts — 18 of which came through Microsoft's commercial field team. They listed on Azure Marketplace and generated $2.8M in Marketplace ARR in their first year. Their Series C was led by a VC who first heard of them at the Hub's Spring Demo Day.

"The co-sell motion opened doors we couldn't have knocked on alone. In six months we went from zero Microsoft customers to 23 enterprise accounts."
Nir Goldstein, CEO, CloudBridge

CyberSentry: 40 Enterprise Co-Sell Deals in 12 Months

$18MSeries A raised
12 mo.Seed to Series A

CyberSentry joined the Hub as a seed-stage company with a compelling zero-trust architecture product but no enterprise sales motion. Through LaunchPad, they received $150K in Azure credits and weekly CTO mentorship from Avi Shapiro that fundamentally reshaped their product architecture to align with Microsoft's security platform roadmap.

After six months in LaunchPad, CyberSentry transitioned to the Partner FastTrack program, where they were introduced to Microsoft's GPS field team and enrolled in a co-sell pilot. The co-sell designation gave their product visibility with Microsoft's enterprise security account teams across EMEA.

CTO Amir Ben-Tzur credits the CTO Circle as much as the co-sell program: "The peer learning from other CTOs who had already navigated enterprise sales cycles saved us from at least three costly mistakes."

Lumina AI: 3× Revenue Growth in 18 Months

3×Revenue growth
€2.2MFirst enterprise deal
6 weeksAI Lab sprint duration

Lumina AI's core product — an AI engine for customer intelligence — was technically impressive but architecturally ill-suited for enterprise deployment. Their Series A had been raised on the strength of the demo; the challenge was building the enterprise-grade version fast enough to close the deals their investors expected.

Six weeks in the AI Innovation Lab with two dedicated Azure AI engineers from Microsoft's ISE team solved this. The team rebuilt Lumina's inference pipeline on Azure OpenAI Service, implemented enterprise security controls, and introduced a scalable multi-tenant architecture that eliminated the biggest technical objection from enterprise buyers.

Lumina AI's first enterprise deal — a €2.2M contract with a major European bank — closed 90 days after the Lab. Their Series B followed 14 months later.

DataFlow Systems: Mature ISV to Azure Marketplace Leader

90 daysTo Marketplace listing
$2.1MFirst-year Marketplace ARR
4×Pipeline expansion

DataFlow Systems had been building real-time data pipeline infrastructure for eight years. Their product worked. Their customers loved it. But the company was stuck — reliant on a legacy distribution model, unable to reach a new generation of cloud-native enterprise buyers.

The ISV Excellence program gave DataFlow a dedicated modernization sprint — three weeks with an ISE architect who rewrote their deployment model for Azure-native delivery. The Partner FastTrack program then prepared their Marketplace listing: offer structure, transactable pricing, and a co-sell designation that put their product in front of Microsoft field teams.

Ninety days after joining the Hub, DataFlow was live on Azure Marketplace. Their first-year Marketplace ARR reached $2.1M — more than double their original forecast.

ShieldLayer: AWS-Born ISV Opens $8M in Azure Pipeline

$8MNew pipeline opened
4 mo.Multi-cloud migration
2 cloudsNow Azure + AWS native

ShieldLayer built a leading cloud security product on AWS over seven years. Their challenge was that their largest prospective buyers — major European enterprises and government entities — had mandatory Azure requirements. They were losing deals not because of their product, but because of their infrastructure.

The ISV Excellence multi-cloud modernization track placed them with an Azure ISE architect who built a complete Azure-native deployment track in parallel with their existing AWS product — no migration, no disruption, just a new go-to-market motion. Within four months, ShieldLayer had Azure as a first-class deployment target and a co-sell designation with Microsoft's security field team.
